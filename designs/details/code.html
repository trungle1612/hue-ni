The Essence

A masterpiece of architectural fusion, the Khai Dinh Tomb stands as a testament to the transition between traditional Vietnamese heritage and Western influence.

Constructed over 11 years, the tomb is perched on the slope of Chau Chu mountain. Its exterior is dark and weathered, contrasting sharply with the opulent, vibrant mosaics of porcelain and glass found within the Thien Dinh Palace.
